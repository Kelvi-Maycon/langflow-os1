# Execution Checklists

## Milestone Quality

Each milestone should answer:

- what outcome is reached
- why that milestone matters
- what must be done before it starts
- how success is verified

## Backlog Quality

A strong backlog item has:

- a specific outcome
- a reason for the work
- dependencies if any
- acceptance criteria
- a clear placement in a phase or workstream

## Risk Review

Look for:

- unclear ownership
- external dependencies
- technical unknowns
- timing assumptions
- resource bottlenecks
- approval or alignment bottlenecks

For each serious risk, add:

- likelihood
- impact
- mitigation
- contingency if mitigation fails

## Metrics

Prefer metrics that show whether the objective improved:

- adoption
- completion rate
- quality
- cycle time
- error rate
- retention
- time saved
- revenue or cost effect

Avoid vanity metrics that do not change decisions.

## Rollout And Review

When rollout matters, include:

- release or handoff steps
- communication needs
- training or enablement
- monitoring after launch
- review checkpoint date

## Open Questions

Keep open questions separate from risks.

Open questions are unresolved facts or decisions.
Risks are things that could go wrong even when the facts are known.
